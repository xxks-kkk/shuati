53. Maximum Subarray¶

This is an reiteration of what I have written previously, i.e., maxSubArray3).

The problem is solved using dynamic programming. Given a sequence of \(k\) numbers: \(nums = \set{n_1, \dots, n_k}\), suppose we have found the maximum subarray sum \(S_i\) for \(n_1, \dots n_i\), we want to find the maximum subarray sum for \(n_1, \dots n_{i+1}\). Then, \(S_{i+1}\) can either be \(S_i\) or the subarray sum that includes \(n_{i+1}\). For the subarray sum that includes \(n_{i+1}\), it can either be the sum of elements in subarray that previously ends at \(n_i\) and now includes \(n_{i+1}\) or the subarray that only includes \(n_{i+1}\), depending on which sum is larger.

In code, we use maxEndingHere to denote the sum of elements in subarray that ends at \(n_i\) and we compute new maxEndingHere based on value of \(n_{i+1}\) as described above, i.e., including \(n_{i+1}\) into maxEndingHere or just use \(n_{i+1}\). Then, we update globalMax, which denotes \(S_i\) previously and \(S_{i+1}\) after the update.
